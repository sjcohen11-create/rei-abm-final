04 — The Three Gaps
New for Spring 2026
The Field Agent
collection.
── ✦ ──
Three instruments built for the terrain your product team crosses every launch. Each one closes a gap your current setup leaves open — and each one is deployed by the people who know the work, not by engineering.
How they're built
Each Field Agent is deployed using Omni — Airtable's plain-language interface that lets your team describe a workflow and have an agent built to run it. No code. No tickets. No waiting on engineering.
Omni prompt example:
"Every time a vendor misses a QC deadline by 48 hours, send them a follow-up email, log the delay in the SKU tracker, and notify Alex."
→ Agent deployed. No dev required.
The Trail Map
Agent-First System of Record
Before you set out, you need to know the terrain. The Trail Map gives your agents structured, current, relational context across every SKU, vendor, and milestone — all connected, all current, all in one place. Your agents stop guessing and start logging.
Coverage: full product lifecycle
Scale: 180+ stores, 100+ vendors
Available in: rain, peak season, global vendor networks
The Base Camp Radio
Built for People Who Know the Work
The people who know the route should be calling it. Deploy and brief agents by describing the task in plain language — no developer, no ticket, no waiting. If your product team can describe a launch milestone, they can build the agent that tracks it.
Range: org-wide
Frequency: zero IT bottlenecks
Compatible with: anyone who's run a product launch
The Summit Scope
Observability and Control
In the field, you need to see what's happening before it becomes a crisis. The Summit Scope defines the boundaries within which agents operate — every update logged, every critical flag visible, every action reversible before it's final. Built for co-op accountability.
Field of view: full agent activity log
Intervention window: before results are final
Accountability: built in — not bolted on after

REI's co-op model means every decision carries member accountability. The Summit Scope is built for exactly that — agents that show their work before it becomes final, so your team can trust what's running.
How they work together on your launch
The Trail Map gives agents the structured context they need. The Base Camp Radio lets your product team deploy them without engineering. The Summit Scope ensures every critical update is logged and every agent action is visible before it becomes final. Together, they turn your Anniversary Sale from a coordination exercise into a reliable, accountable system — run by the people who know the launch best.
